# Product roadmap

## Product promise

The Surf Log helps longboarders collect, remember, and plan around soft waves worth traveling for.

It is a dream map and personal log for people who still love a clean little wall.

Not Surfline. Not a forecast app. Not social media. Not travel booking.

## Layer 1 — The Log

Personal record of waves.

V1 starts local-only:

- Want to hit
- Hit it
- Draft wave suggestions

Future optional fields:

- Date surfed
- Board ridden
- Who with
- Session note
- Photo/memory
- Favorite / would return

## Layer 2 — The Map

Visual dream-list orientation.

Core questions:

- Where are the soft waves worth planning around?
- What have I hit?
- What do I still want to hit?
- What clusters into a trip?

Map should support:

- Pin states: unmarked / want / hit
- List-map sync
- Approximate public/famous break coordinates only
- No secret-spot precision

## Layer 3 — Optional account

Do not make accounts the front door.

Account prompt should appear only after a user has created value locally, e.g. after marking 3+ waves:

```text
Save your log across devices
```

Account-backed features:

- Sync want/hit list across devices
- Private notes
- Export/share read-only log
- Future trip lists

## Layer 4 — Trip planner

The trip planner is likely the long-term product wedge.

Prompt examples:

```text
I want a soft longboard trip in March.
Warm water preferred.
Intermediate, not crowded if possible.
7–10 days.
```

Inputs:

- Travel month/window
- Skill level
- Warm/cold water preference
- Wave direction preference
- Mellow/dreamy vs iconic/pilgrimage
- Family/partner-friendly town needed?
- Distance/budget tolerance
- Crowds tolerance

Outputs:

- Suggested destination clusters
- Why it fits
- Season fit
- Crowd caveat
- Skill caveat
- Nearby backup waves
- Add-to-trip action

Example destination clusters:

- Oaxaca / Saladita / Troncones / Punta Mita style Mexico options
- Waikiki longboard week
- Noosa / Byron / Crescent Head Australia lane
- Costa Rica / Nicaragua mellow warm-water lane

## Product boundaries

- Do not claim live conditions or safety certainty.
- Do not reveal secret spots.
- Do not optimize for chargers/performance surfing.
- Do not become another noisy surf content platform.
- Keep the object quiet, useful, and travel-dream oriented.
